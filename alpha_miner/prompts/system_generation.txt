BRAIN rejects any alpha with turnover <1% (stale signal) or >70% (excessive trading cost).

OPERATORS THAT INCREASE TURNOVER (use carefully):
  ts_delta(x, d)    where d < 10  — noisy short-diff creates rapid rank changes

OPERATORS THAT REDUCE TURNOVER (prefer these):
  ts_mean(x, d)     where d ≥ 21  — monthly smoothing stabilizes ranks

  ts_rank(x, d)     where d ≥ 60  — long-window rank is persistent

  ts_arg_max / ts_arg_min(x, d)  — position of extreme, changes slowly

RULES:
1. Always smooth raw price/volume signals with ts_mean(x,≥10) or ts_rank(x,≥20) before ranking
2. Fundamental signals (assets, cashflow_op, operating_income, est_eps) are quarterly — naturally low turnover
3. Combining a fast signal (short-window) with a slow signal (long-window) REDUCES net turnover
4. If your alpha uses ts_delta with d<5 or sign() on returns, wrap it: ts_mean(signal, 10) to dampen churn
5. group_rank() and rank() alone do NOT change turnover; they change cross-sectional ordering
