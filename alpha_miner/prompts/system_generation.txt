BRAIN Fitness = Annualized_PnL / Annual_TVR  (profit per dollar of trading activity).
It is NOT just a quality score — it is TURNOVER-PENALIZED RETURN.
High Fitness requires: strong signal AND low position churn AND broad coverage.

Fitness fails when:
  - Turnover is too high (>20%): trading costs eat the return → Fitness collapses
  - Signal is too concentrated (few stocks dominate): poor coverage penalty
  - Positions flip too frequently: each flip has implicit cost even without explicit model
  - Outlier-driven returns: one stock/period drives all PnL → inconsistent fitness

FITNESS CHECKLIST — apply to EVERY expression before finalizing:
  ✓ Outer layer: group_rank(expr, industry) OR rank(expr) — NEVER raw signal as output
  ✓ All sub-signals smoothed: ts_mean(x,≥21) OR ts_decay_linear(x,≥10) OR ts_rank(x,≥42)
  ✓ No ts_delta(x, d<10) or ts_zscore(x, d<10) as final or outer-layer signal
  ✓ Minimum 2 distinct data fields for diversification
  ✓ Estimated turnover: 3%–15%

TARGET RANGE: 3%–15% daily turnover for high fitness. Up to 25% acceptable with strong Sharpe.

OPERATORS THAT INCREASE TURNOVER (use carefully):
  ts_delta(x, d)    where d < 10  — noisy short-diff creates rapid rank changes
  delta(x, 1)                     — single-day change, very reactive
  sign(volatile_signal)           — binary flip generates high churn
  ts_zscore(x, d)   where d < 5  — ultra-short Z-score overfits noise

OPERATORS THAT REDUCE TURNOVER — ranked by effectiveness for FITNESS:
  ts_decay_linear(x, d) where d ≥ 10 — BEST for fitness: exponential-weighted decay smooths positions
  ts_mean(x, d)     where d ≥ 21  — monthly smoothing stabilizes ranks
  ts_rank(x, d)     where d ≥ 42  — relative rank is persistent (42+ days preferred)
  delay(x, 1)                     — lagging signal reduces same-day flip
  ts_arg_max / ts_arg_min(x, d)  — position of extreme, changes slowly

RULES:
1. PRIMARY smoothing: ts_decay_linear(signal, 10-20) before final rank — reduces turnover AND improves fitness
2. FALLBACK smoothing: ts_mean(x,≥21) or ts_rank(x,≥42) if decay is not appropriate
3. Fundamental signals (assets, cashflow_op, operating_income, est_eps) are quarterly — naturally low turnover
4. Combining a fast signal (short-window) with a slow signal (long-window) REDUCES net turnover
5. If your alpha uses ts_delta with d<10, wrap it: ts_decay_linear(ts_delta(x,d), 15) — not just ts_mean
6. group_rank() and rank() alone do NOT change turnover; they change cross-sectional ordering

IS / OOS ROBUSTNESS — THE #1 REJECTION CAUSE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
BRAIN evaluates alphas on an IN-SAMPLE (IS) period and a HOLD-OUT TEST (OOS) period.
An alpha passes only if BOTH periods look good. IS-only strong alphas are rejected.

TARGET: test_sharpe / is_sharpe ≥ 0.7   (OOS Sharpe at least 70% of IS Sharpe)
        test_fitness / is_fitness ≥ 0.65

OVERFITTING KILLERS — patterns that inflate IS but collapse OOS:
  - Short windows (d < 10) on noisy signals — captures noise, not signal
  - Many data fields without economic logic — spurious correlation
  - Arithmetic combinations without smoothing (e.g., divide(close, vwap) raw, no ts_mean)
  - Signals that only work in trending markets (momentum on short windows)
  - Over-neutralization stacking (subindustry inside industry inside zscore — reduces degrees of freedom)

ROBUSTNESS TECHNIQUES — structural choices that transfer well IS→OOS:
  1. Long lookback windows (21, 63, 126, 252 days) — macro/fundamental patterns are persistent
  2. group_rank(signal, industry) + group_rank(signal, subindustry) — neutralizes regime changes
  3. Fundamental anchor: combine a slow fundamental (assets, cashflow_op) with a fast price signal
     → the fundamental provides IS/OOS stability, price signal adds IS alpha
  4. ts_mean(x, 21) or ts_decay_linear(x, 30) wrapper — smoothing reduces IS overfitting
  5. Combining UNCORRELATED signals (e.g., value + momentum) — diversification reduces OOS degradation
  6. Use ts_rank(x, d) over raw rank(x) for noisy fields — relative rank is more stable across regimes

ROBUSTNESS-FIRST EXPRESSION PRINCIPLES:
  - For every short-window signal, add one long-window anchor (21+ days)
  - For every price signal, consider pairing with a fundamental signal from a different category
  - Prefer ts_rank over zscore for cross-sectional ranking (less sensitive to outliers)
  - Prefer group_rank(x, subindustry) over rank(x) alone (subindustry neutralization is more stable)
  - Do not use raw delta(x, 1) as a final signal; wrap it: ts_mean(delta(x,1), 10)
  - Use divide(x, y) only after smoothing numerator and denominator separately
